5a-b-c
by Paul

Once again being involved in the design of the 5B's and 5C's I can tell you that the of course the 5B's came out after the 5A's and the 5C's after that. The 5B's (wer'nt they a terrible design, Im embarresed to admit my work on them) were replace by the 5C's mostly because of the poor design, they were really just a stop gap, the military wanted to use a battery available anywhere in the world, therefore the AA battery.
The 5B's will not fit the 5C case with out major modification. As was noted the difference in lenses also from unit to unit. CT Way too bright
by jimbo

for most gen2 and all gen3 equipment. They are the brightest mass market aiming lasers and a great way to burn your tube.

Laser max are much better as they are a lot dimmer and are pulsed, but they are still bright enough to cause streaking out to about 15 feet.

Your best bet is something that is variable power, or if you don't mind tinkering, you can cover the laser with a piece of a neutral density filter from a camera or camcorder filter. Tinkering like that does cause some heat buildup with prolonged use and of course with the filter in place, the laser is too dim for daylight use.

You might try a Doctor Optic holosight. I've used this setup with PVS7D's at Thunder Ranch and it worked out very well (the dot brightness adjusts with ambient light levels).

by PAUL

If this is the same head mount as the 7B, then yes if you are careful it can be adjusted. I just got a D300 with headband and it is terrible. As one of the designers of the 7B I can tell you Uncle Sam would never accept this.
If you remove the black epoxy from the inside of the mount and loosen the nut,slide the mount to the rear (close to headmount) then install your socket (at Varo this was known as the pecker) and firmly hold in the locked position. Now with a pointy something pull the catch to the rear as much as you can and re-tighten the nut, really requires three hands.Apply new epoxy.
At Varo there is a special tool that sets each catch bar, you simply plug it into the front, it moves the catch where it should be and then they tighten the nut, apply epoxy, remove the pecker and it's done.
I have done this several time and will have to do it to the D300 also.
Now if you screw up and get it cocked sideways or something you could be in this adjustment mode for several tries. BE CAREFUL
