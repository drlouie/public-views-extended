re: dusting optics
by drtink

I have been involved with photography for many years and have probably used every lens cleaning gizmo since the 1970's. The best products by far are the Carson Lenspen and microfiber cloths (the finer the weave, the better). Both are available at Wolf, Ritz, NYC stores such as B and H or Adorama, or any well-stocked local store. Lenspen is also sold as a scope accessory by Leupold. Before using either one, any loose material should be removed from the surface using canned air or a CLEAN SOFT camel's hair brush. A cleaning solution may be used sparingly, but with the more volatile solutions there is at least the potential hazard of their seeping into an optical device and damaging cemented lens elements. I usually use the cloth after breathing on the lens to fog it and have found this method very effective for removing fingerprints and other smudges. Keep the cloth in a plastic bag and hand wash as needed with a mild soap and it should last almost indefinitely. Delicate surfaces such as front-surface mirrors (like SLR camera mirrors) should not be touched with ANYTHING other than air from a bulb syringe-- no canned air. On the subject of canned air- don't shake the can and don't invert it. One more thing-- a small amount of dust on a lens will not affect the image quality; I suspect that Lou Boyd will confirm this last statement. Hope this helps.
