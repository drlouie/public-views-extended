IR filter to prevent bright light damage?
by

An IR filter can pass infrared light while blocking almost all visible light. The problem is that most commercial light sources (mercury, sodium, and incandescent) still have quite a bit of infrared withing the sensitivity range of the intensifier. SO no, it won't work. Sure, it will help some.

A better solution is to put a longer focal length lens on your NV device so you can more selectivly look at the low brightness areas without having a bright direct light in the intensifier's field of view. You see more detail too.

Tube removal.
by

Once you have removed the eyepiece assy (with tube), you can look down the sided of the tube to the base. There should be two silver/gold "halves" that surround the base of the tube. They are held tight by the four tiny allen screws. You need to loosen these four allen screws, and the "leaves" will fall out. then you can unscrew the tube, and screw the new one in place. Don't over-torque the allen screws, or the tube when you screw either one in place, as the threads are very fine and will strip easily. These two leaves have edges that fit into the V-shaped slot at the base of the tube. This holds the tube tight once it is threaded in the housing.
