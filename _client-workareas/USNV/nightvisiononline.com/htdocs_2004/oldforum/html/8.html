Gen 1 vs Gen 1+
by

99% of the so called 'Gen 1+' are nothing but hype. The other 1% of real Russian Gen 1+ are:
a) 2 stage gen 1 tubes. Not in production for a while. Were used on the following models: Baigish 5P, NZT 3B, NP 2, T3H and maybe few more. Easy to identify due to long size of the tube, and consequently - the product.
b) Special Gen 1 tubes with fibre optic washer. Results in very good resolution (up to 50 lp/mm), with no 'fish eye' effect. Russian name for this tube: 'KARAT'.
Used in a special version of ON 1-20 NV Goggles (never seen in USA), DN 200 weapon scopes (we've sold it few years back). It's in a very limited production, and devices using it will cost over 600$

Way back when...
by

I first saw anything on so called "gen 4" tubes, the specs were not so amazingly better than a real good (standard today)gen 3 filmed tube. There are a few aspects of the filmless that are better but the price difference involved is really not worth it. Also there is that nagging question of reliability and longevity. They are not readily available anyway, so it is sort of a moot discussion. Thin film come available on occasion, not often, and usually not the best quality is available to the commercial market if any are. MX9916 repoting
by DavidS

I personnaly repoted a MX9916 tube without any experience before nor any help.
First you have to dissable the tube with X-acto knofe. be carefull to not cut any parts. do it slowly. then I have separated the tube himself and the plastic ase. to remove the silicone, it is easy with some tweezers and the X-acto. Take care of the wires.with a modeling screw driver, I have dissable the two electronics compartments, desoldered the wires.be sure to remove all the silicone, the only way is to scrape it all with the X-acto knife and the tweezers
I changed the unworkable parts n, resoldered the wires and put the juice "on". it was working but arcing a lot.
firstly,I put a large quantity of Superglue( Cyanoacrylate) on each wires. this glue is a very good isolating material. once it is dry, I get a silicone tube as what you can find for waterproofing in Bathrooms and Kitchens.I put larges quantities too in the plastic case and all arround the tube. it's not important there is to much silicone and that it overflows because it is the only way to be sure you will have silicone wrap around the tube. once the silicone is dry, with the X-acto you can remove all that overflows.you can clean all the surface with a smooth cloth and some methylated spirit .
when I tested the tube, there was a" leak" of High voltage just close to the photocatode. just a very little point and that affected the tube working when I touch it( the screen turned to black, I was grounding the electical power) I just applied a point of superglue when it leaks and all went in perfect order. the tube works perfectly.

of course it should be a "official" way to do it with better or specifics material I didn't use, but I did it with easy to find materials and It works: that was the wanted end after all.

PAS-5
by

The Israeli 5151 is almost the same as the US AN/PAS-5 Gen O IR goggles.
I think STANO is the onlys ource of parts and repairs of these now.
You will need an IR light source to use these.

We used to sell lots of these units years ago...you do not want to experience having an electrical short in the face mask / powersupply area! Which they were prone to do.

Most people confuse the battery and the power supply.
The battery is an Eveready PX-1...the powersupply is well...scarce. It is silver and shaped like a roll of dimes that goes under the power supply.

The way the US military de-milled the PAS-5's was to take out all the power supplies, put them on a pile in a parking lot, and call in a tank....

They are useless without an infrared light source.
Of course...even these units can not be exported!

Surplused vs stolen vs used vs units made from parts
by


There are surplussed units out there up to and including PVS-14s, but they are usually restricted to law enforcement under DRMO or certain lend/lease programs. Once they have been in posession of LE for a while, the LE agency may surplus them out. I do not know of any other units that get surplused from the military to the open market without passing through some intermediary user.

Stolen gear will show signs of tampering, or will simply say "Property of US Government" right on the side. The PVS-14s that my unit issues have it embedded in the housing in white which appears to go all the way through, so if you scratch it, the marking remains. I really don't understand how something like that can change hands on ebay and not be tracked. You have to have a legitimate ID and credit card to get an Ebay account, and the packages are usually insured and tracked. Again, not so anonymous and it would seem that such things would be easy to track.

There are used units around where people tossed the paperwork, but there will usually be something different about them. Metal vs plastic fittings, different kind of case (like the stuff that comes from US Nightvision comes in black cases). Lots of commercial PVS-7s have a different front lens.

There are also plenty of legitimate units with no paperwork that were made from parts. Some new parts combined with surplus, damaged, or used parts to come up with functioning units.

Personally, when I am in the market for the used stuff, I will gravitate toward civilian equivalent gear just so some jealous officer or staff NCO does not get jealous and call it stolen. This is a particularly bad situation in the Marines where lots of legitimately owned private gear is considered stolen until proven otherwise. Note that situation is different between someone living under UCMJ and someone under civilian law where the burden of proof is different.

FOCUS
by PAUL

Yes that is about right, the focus is set up to go just thru infinity, otherwise you would never know if it were focused right.Soliders, and others, need to see it go just out of focus or they would keep trying to twist the lens further. This is the only reason it is set up this way.
To tell if someone has set the focus ring wrong try this. Remove the objective from the goggle. Put a circular piece of paper over the fron of the tube and then put just the smallest amount of "O-LUBE" or some other grease on the rear of the objective lens METAL housing.Do not put the grease on the glass, now when you turn the lens all the way in it will leave a mark on the paper if it is bottoming out. Originally there was about .010-.015 clearance ot the rear of the lens-to-glass.
The focus knob on the objective and the focus set ring on the objective body are glued on with EC-801 compound and can be difficult to readjust.
