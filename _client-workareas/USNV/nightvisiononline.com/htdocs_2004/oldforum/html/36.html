Omnibus
by

contracts are the govt specification requirement contracts for suppliers. I am not sure what it means, but I think it has something to do with all-encompassing. The contracts cover the requirements for a set period of time and minimum specs and quantities for everything that they buy or require. That may not be exactly correct, but it will give you an idea. As pertaining to NV, they set the minimum specs required, and also the quanities required from each vendor. The MX prefix tubes are military spec tubes, and the F and M prefix are ITT and Litton commercial. However, it does not mean all that much anymore, since the commercial labels can be mil-spec as well. The image tube labelling has become quite un-interpretable unless you are on the inside, and as with ITT stuff, I don't think even the insiders know what they all mean.

Basically there isn't a whole lot to choose between any of the Gen I monoculars. I like the Dipol D-3MK myself because it is very small, light, has a robust quality of construction and can be mounted on a head mount to provide hands free viewing.
