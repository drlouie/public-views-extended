There are basically two types of reticles...
by RJ

1) ...reticles mounted infront of the NV tube.

2) ...reticles mounted in the eye piece.

The concensus of experienced users suggest the type 1 option provides the most accuracy becuase it eleimantes eye piece parallax.

However, if you are a solid shooter with a traditional daylight scope, you should not have any problem with a rear eye piece mounted reticle.

The last thing to consider in both types of reticles... not all systems are created equal. Another words, a Raptor reticle by design can handle more abuse than an ATN NV reticle. This comparison may also serve to demonstrate both end of the quality spectrum, although there are better systems than the Raptor as well as worse systems than ATN.

That being said, I have an illuminated eye peice reticle for my ATN Patriot monocular, and it has been a very solid performer. But their other weapon sight systems have mixed reviews with a larger share of negative feedback from respectable NV sources.

rear cover gasket
by PAUL

For those of you wanting the information on the rear cover o-ring here it is:
P/N: MS28775-044: NSN: 5330-01-098-1232.
If you are aquianted with the MS sys then you can easily find this,otherwise here is the description:
O-ring: I.D.=3.739 +/- .027
Width= .070 +/-.003
Nominal size: 3 3/4 I.D. x 3 7/8 O.D.x 1/16 Thk. Rember o-ring sizes are given by the Inside Dia not the Outside Dia.
All o-rings are not equal, this is/was a BUNA N (Nitrile) o-ring. It should be lubricated very lightly with SUPER O-LUBE grease. Not all grease is compatible with all o-ring material, if you dont have any super o-lube I recommend using nothing, since this is a static load anyway.
When installing the o-ring make sure that you do not "roll" (twist) the ring as it goes into the groove.
Where to get one ? Around here I go the local bearing supply house, many times they will just give you one. Motion Industries is a good source if there is one near you.
