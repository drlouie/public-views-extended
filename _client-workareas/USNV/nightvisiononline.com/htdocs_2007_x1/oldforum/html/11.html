Texas Hunting
by

I am from the San Antonio area of Texas and hunting is about all I do. I have been a hunting guide for over 7 years since I retired from the Air Force. Our hunting seasons start in October which is the Archery only season and mostly it is for white tail deer and turkeys. Rifle season starts in November and runs thru January for the same type of game. Muzzle loader is allowed for 2 weeks after that. You may bow hunt October through January if you want. These seasons are are state regulated and you have various amounts of tags depending on the county where you hunt. Where I hunt I can harvest 2 whitetail bucks, 3 does and 3 gooblers. Most all of the state of Texas is privately owned, therefor you must lease some hunting land or if your lucky like me and are retired military you can hunt on military land. There are also a lot of exotic animal that were intoduced many years ago and you may hunt for them anytime day or night since they do not have to be tagged. Animals such as feral hogs, axis deer, sika deer,aoudad, and nilgai antelope to name a few. Most exotics are on game ranches (high fences) but some have escaped over the years and seem to turn up in strange places. The only animals you can legally hunt with night vision equipment are these pigs and exotics. Javelina are also huntable year round but you may only harvest 2 per year. I bowhunt year round but do a lot of hog hunting at night with my night vision scope. Have taken well over 400 hogs between 5 of us in the past 2 years. I go to Colorado every year to bow hunt Elk and Black bear. High fenced game ranches here in Texas can be big dollars. When I guided on the 777 Ranch in Hondo some of their whitetails went for $25,000. Well this has turned into a letter so I hope you get some idea of hunting here. Tom

TVS-2 power supply
by

I didn't answer when I first saw your post because I don't have direct experience with the TVS-2.

I do have a TVS-4 and I believe it uses the same power supply. Check out this ad on ebay and see if the description matches the one you have. This does sound like the unit used in the TVS-4 and may be the one in the TVS-2. It's just a little solid state oscillator which take 6 volts (nominal) and produces about 2KV of high frequency AC. This is fed to the potted image tube which has high voltage diode/capacitor multipliers for each stage (anode and focus voltages).

http://cgi.ebay.com/ws/eBayISAPI.dll?ViewItem&item=739133505

The following website has the manual for the army test set used to check batteries and shows the same battery
(BA-1100/U) is used is both the TVS-2 and TVS-4):
http://www.pacificsites.com/~brooke/TS183.shtml
Note that it shows the >minimum< voltage for this unit
as 5 volts.

I run my TVS-4 on four penlite cells and have also run it on two 3 volt lithium coin cells.

I don't know if the power supply is reverse polarity prtected, so be careful.

About the o'ring
by

If the groove for the o'ring is cut into the flat surfaces that slide together, one on top of another, then it's cut for an o'ring.
If it's cut into the vertical falat surface where the units mate after they are slid together, then it doesn't accomodate an o'ring.

In other words, if the back slides into the unit so the o'ring is caught between the sliding surfaces, the it takes an o'ring. If the back slides on and then the sealing surfaces press against each other, then it's not an o'ring setup. You'll have to use RTB.

state to state NV laws
by

Most state laws don't say much about night vision gear directly. I don't know of state where you can't own a passive viewer. There are some states with laws preventing the mounting of active NV scopes on firearms (California). There are also differences between states with respect to using night vision gear for hunting, whether it's mounted to a firearm or not. Some aren't well defined, such as whether an infrared illuminator is considered a light. Some states have the wording that you can't hunt after dark. Some that you can't use a "light". What constitutes "dark" may be up to a game warden, judge, or jury. Your argument that it wasn't too dark to see with your Gen III scope may not go very far.

If in doubt, consult your state laws or write a letter to your state's Attorney General with your specific question. If you have a letter from your AG it might keep you out of jail. Then again it might not.
