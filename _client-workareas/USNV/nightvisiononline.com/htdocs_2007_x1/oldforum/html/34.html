PVS-4
by PAUL

If you remove the focus lock ring, the outer ring that limits the travel of the eyepiece, from the unit then you can unscrew the entire eyepiece-tube assembly.
Inside ayou will find the split ring mentioned below, this is held in by four set screws. It may be loose if so this can cause the tube to "unscrew" from the assembly.
The tube is actually screwed into the eyepiece assembly and then this split ring is screwed against the tube as a locking feature. This allows you to focus by movint the tube in and out, away from the objective. The tube is always screwed into the assembly untill it bottoms out, then the split ring is installed.
The hard part now is re-focusing the tube. When you put it all back together,BEFORE, you replace the outer lock ring you have to focus the tube to infinity,no do not look at the stars and focus, you really need a test bench, or you can use the military alternate test method. When everything is focused, then replace the outer lock ring, the one that limits travel of the eyepiece.

It's on it's way out, I'll give you $50 for it :-)
by Sid

I wouldn't know the insides of a PVS-4 from a hole in the ground, but experience from repairing other NV equipment and day optics suggests that the O-ring isn't relevant to your problem.

All ( objective ) focus adjustments on weaponsights that I have worked on are preloaded by either a coil spring or a 'wavy washer' ( belleville spring ) to take out the inevitable backlash in the threads that comes from manufacturing tolerances and wear.

I think maybe you have a tired spring, or more likely a thread thats worn in one place from spending most of it's life being rotated around one particular area.
I do know some dodges for re-parallaxing day scopes to put the worn area into a range you wouldn't usually be focussing at, but I'm out of my depth with cool milspec gear like PVS-4's :-)

Hope it doesn't spoil your enjoyment of the scope, it sounds like it is one excellent piece of kit, as we say in England :-)

KOS
by

The Kite weapon sight is one form of the observation system (KOS) that was originally made by Pilkington.
First models were manufactured about 16 years ago, and the design has not changed much since. (I dont know how long the raptor or acquila are about).
The Kite comes in a number of different formats Gen 2, Gen 2+, Supergen and Gen 3, only the Gen 2+ and less are available for sale to civilians. I have used the Gen 2+ and Gen 3, and both are excellent. The Gen 2+ gives excellent resolution and contrast. The reason why this sight has been so successful (apart from the tubes) is the excellent body - it is very tough, but very light (impact resistant plastic) and is completely "soldier proof". It comes with a dedicated receiver to fit the customers weapon, and despite being a little high, it allows the user to get a very comfortable firing position, and does not unbalance natural pointing to any major degree. In trials, which also included an earlier version of the PVS-4, everybody preferred the Kite because it handled well, was simple to use, and didnt leave a trail of pieces of itself behind the user !
To US NV users, yes it definitely is more expensive then domestic equivalents, and so probably of little interest to you - but when the government is paying the asking price, you could do a lot worse !
