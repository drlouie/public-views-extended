502

I have the same 502.
The battery cover earths the negatives to the housing via the screw.
I put a tape over the battery cover to isolate the cover from the batteries then I insert a copper strip between the battery negatives and the cover for each chamber taking care that the copper DOESN'T touch the housing.
In one chamber I put in 2 N type giving 3 V, and the other a AAA with an insulated metal extension giving 1.5V.

Now I have a 2 step variable gain 502.

potting
by Woody

your intensifier assymbly consists of three components. The intensifier tube, the power supply (around the tube), and the boot or sleeve that it is "potted" in. the power supply is soldered and then bonded to and at the same time insulated from the tube and also encapsulated with an RTV type silicon (that contains corrosion inhibitors) into the boot or sleeve. Your problem sounds like there has been a failure of either the bonding or insulating ability of the silicon due to moisture, age, vibration, etc. .Repotting your tube would consist of removing the tube and power supply from the sleeve or boot. testing each seperate component replacing any defective parts and then re-encapsulating the tube and power supply.
